SIT223-SIT753 - Professional Practice in IT

Unit Details

Year: 2023 unit information
Enrolment modes: Trimester 1: Burwood (Melbourne), Geelong, Cloud (online)
Credit point(s): 1
Unit Chair: Trimester 1: Azadeh Ghari Neiat

Unit content

Introduction to Professionalism in IT

    Definition of professionalism in the IT context
    Importance of professionalism in the IT industry
    Ethical considerations and codes of conduct

IT Industry Overview

    Understanding the structure of the IT industry
    Key roles and responsibilities within IT organizations
    Trends and emerging technologies in the IT sector

Ethics and Legal Framework

    Ethical considerations in IT decision-making
    Legal frameworks relevant to IT professionals (e.g., data protection laws, intellectual property rights)
    Case studies and ethical dilemmas in IT

Communication Skills

    Importance of effective communication in IT projects
    Techniques for clear and concise communication
    Communicating technical information to non-technical stakeholders

Teamwork and Collaboration

    Strategies for effective teamwork in IT projects
    Building and leading high-performing IT teams
    Conflict resolution and problem-solving within teams

Project Management Principles

    Introduction to project management methodologies (e.g., Agile, Waterfall)
    Project planning, scheduling, and budgeting
    Risk management and mitigation strategies

Professional Development

    Continuous learning and skill development in IT
    Networking and building professional relationships
    Career advancement strategies and opportunities in the IT industry

Case Studies and Real-World Examples

    Analyzing real-world scenarios and case studies related to professional practice in IT
    Drawing lessons from both successful and unsuccessful IT projects
    Guest lectures or industry speakers to provide insights from practitioners

Assessment and Evaluation

    Assessments may include individual and group projects, presentations, case studies, exams, and reflective reports.
    Evaluation criteria may focus on professionalism, ethical decision-making, communication skills, teamwork, and application of knowledge to real-world situations.
